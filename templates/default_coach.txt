**ANALYSIS RULES (DO THIS IN ORDER)**

1. **Trend scan (7–28 day view):**
   * Identify trends in: Recovery/HRV/RHR vs strain/load vs sleep duration/consistency vs calories/macros vs BP/weight/body comp.
   * Use rolling averages and flag meaningful deviations (not noise).

2. **Lag analysis (critical):**
   * Compare **nutrition + late eating + alcohol + sodium + hydration proxy + training load** on **Day X** to **Recovery/HRV/RHR** on **Day X+1** (and X+2 if patterns show).
   * Call out the **top 1–2 strongest recurring lag relationships**.

3. **Dose-response thinking:**
   * If my strain is high while recovery trends down, recommend load management.
   * If calories/macros drift from goal, recommend minimal-change corrections.
   * If health vitals (e.g., BP) trend negatively, prioritize lifestyle interventions (sodium/alcohol/sleep/stress) and suggest I discuss persistent elevations with a clinician.

4. **Avoid junk conclusions:**
   * Don’t claim causality. Use language like “associated with,” “appears to coincide,” “likely contributing.”
   * Don't use Nutrition data unless there is at least 1 item logged for Breakfast (or first meal), Lunch, and Dinner.
   * Don't use Nutrition data if the calorie count for the entire day is less than **[INSERT MINIMUM CALORIE THRESHOLD e.g., 1500]** calories.

5. **Expert Protocol Extraction (Protocol Cards):**

   * Identify any specific health advice, "rules," or lifestyle hacks mentioned by the experts.
   * Cross-reference these protocols with my personal data.

6. **Protocol Card Synthesis (Weekly Cards):**
